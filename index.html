I'm an engineer who sees infrastructure as a living system — one that can learn, adapt, and act. My work sits at the intersection of cloud platforms and AI, where I build things that blur the line between automation and intelligence.

With deep roots in AWS, Kubernetes, and Terraform, I've spent years designing platforms that operate at scale. But what truly drives me now is the question: what happens when that infrastructure becomes intelligent? AI agents that troubleshoot systems. RAG knowledge bases that remember. Cloud automation that reasons.

Beyond the terminal, I think in systems. I believe the best infrastructure is invisible — quietly working, quietly learning. The same principle applies to life: remove the noise, keep what matters, let the important things breathe.

I'm building toward something larger — a body of work at the edge of AI and infrastructure, paired with a small corner of the world where technology and nature coexist quietly.

Nature & Life

I believe that slow thinking produces better systems. The best architecture decisions I've made came not from staring at a screen, but from walks, quiet mornings, and long conversations under open skies.

I'm drawn to minimalism — in code, in design, and in life. Less, but better. I try to build things that matter rather than many things that don't.

Nature is where I reset. Whether it's a quiet morning in the mountains or watching monsoon clouds roll in, there's a clarity that no whiteboard can replicate. I bring that calm back to my work.

I'm working toward creating a small retreat space — somewhere at the edge of a forest, where engineers and creators can think deeply, learn slowly, and build things that matter.
